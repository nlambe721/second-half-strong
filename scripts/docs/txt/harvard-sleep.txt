Design Thinking: Root Causes of the Global Sleep Epidemic
🎯 Empathy & Define Phase: Understand the Real Problem
Despite increasing awareness of sleep’s importance, sleep deprivation continues to rise due to systemic, behavioral, and cultural issues.
💥 The Deep Root Causes:
1️⃣ Hyperconnected, Always-On Culture
Technology (screens, social media, work-from-home) exposes us to blue light and disrupts melatonin production.
Notifications and digital overload create mental stimulation late into the night.
Work creep: People work longer hours or check emails late, blurring work-life boundaries.
2️⃣ Stress Epidemic + Nervous System Dysregulation
Chronic stress, anxiety, and mental fatigue spike cortisol and activate the sympathetic nervous system (fight-or-flight).
Many people live in constant hyper-arousal, unable to “shut down” at night.
3️⃣ Circadian Misalignment
We live indoor, artificial-light-based lives that disconnect us from sunlight, our strongest circadian anchor.
People wake up without sun exposure and stay up late with artificial lighting, suppressing melatonin and disrupting the biological clock.
Shift work, jet lag, and poor sleep timing misalign natural rhythms.
4️⃣ Band-Aid Solutions Over Root Causes
People turn to sleeping pills, melatonin, or tech devices instead of addressing behavior, light exposure, timing, and nervous system regulation.
“Sleep hygiene lists” lack personalization, prioritization, and long-term impact.
5️⃣ Lack of Sleep Education + Healthcare Fragmentation
Most health coaches, trainers, and physicians receive no formal training in sleep.
Sleep is siloed from other domains (nutrition, fitness, mental health), when in fact it connects to everything.
3 P’s Model of Insomnia
The 3 P’s Model of Insomnia is a powerful framework for understanding how sleep problems begin and become chronic. Let’s break down each “P”—Predisposing, Precipitating, and Perpetuating Factors—and apply it directly to sleep challenges, especially for men over 40 (though it applies universally).
🧬 1️⃣ Predisposing Factors
These are the underlying traits, conditions, or genetics that make someone more vulnerable to sleep issues.
They don’t directly cause insomnia, but they set the stage.
🔍 Examples (as it relates to sleep):
High-stress sensitivity / Type A personality
History of anxiety, depression, or trauma
Poor stress regulation / overactive nervous system
Genetic predisposition to light sleep
Irregular circadian rhythm or natural night owl
Childhood exposure to poor sleep habits
💡 Think of this as your internal "soil"—some people are more prone to sleep struggles depending on what's already under the surface.
⚡ 2️⃣ Precipitating Factors
These are the trigger events or changes that initiate the sleep problem.
They’re often temporary, but they push the system out of balance.
🔍 Examples:
Acute stress (divorce, job loss, illness, financial worries)
Major life transitions (retirement, having a child, becoming a caregiver)
Injury or pain flare-up
Travel / jet lag / time zone changes
Shift work or schedule disruption
Pandemic-related stress or routine breakdown
💥 These are the "events" that tip someone from normal sleep into disordered sleep patterns—even if temporarily.
🔄 3️ Perpetuating Factors
These are the behaviors, beliefs, and environmental conditions that maintain or worsen the sleep issue over time, even after the trigger has passed.
This is where most chronic insomnia lives—because the body starts associating the bedroom or bedtime with anxiety, frustration, or poor sleep.
🔍 Examples:
Poor sleep hygiene habits (screens before bed, irregular wake times, caffeine late in day)
Trying to “catch up” on sleep with naps or weekend oversleeping
Staying in bed while awake (creates negative association with bed)
Overthinking sleep (“I HAVE to sleep or I’ll be exhausted!”)
Increased worry about consequences of poor sleep
Reliance on alcohol, melatonin, or sleep meds without solving root cause
Inactivity during the day / sedentary lifestyle
💡 Perpetuating factors are like pouring gasoline on a fire that was originally started by something else. You keep the problem alive—even when the original issue is gone.

🧠 Why This Model Matters:
If you want to help someone truly resolve their sleep issues, you must:
Acknowledge and accept the predisposing factors (you can't always change these, but you can work with them)
Identify and manage the original trigger (stress, pain, etc.)
Break the cycle of perpetuation with education, coaching, behavior change, and sometimes CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia)

Why Focus on Perpetuating Factors?
Because by the time someone is asking for help, the predisposing and precipitating factors have already happened or are out of their control.
But perpetuating factors are:
🔁 Daily behaviors
🧠 Mindset traps
🔦 Environmental conditions
💥 Habits that keep the cycle of poor sleep alive
This is where coaching makes the biggest difference.
🧠 Examples of Perpetuating Factors to Coach Through:
1️⃣ Sleep Anxiety / Over-Focus on Sleep
❌ "I HAVE to sleep 8 hours or I’ll feel terrible."
❌ "I’m broken because I can’t sleep like I used to."
🛠️ Coach Reframe:
👉 "Your body is designed to heal. The goal is not perfect sleep—it's consistent rhythm and recovery-supporting behavior."
2️⃣ Inconsistent Wake Times
❌ Waking up at different times each day (especially sleeping in on weekends).
🛠️ Coach Fix:
✅ Set a non-negotiable wake-up time (even if the night was rough). This re-anchors the circadian rhythm.
3️⃣ Lying in Bed Awake
❌ Tossing, turning, checking the clock = creates frustration and anxiety around sleep.
🛠️ Coach Tip:
✅ If awake for more than 15-20 minutes, get up, go to another room, and do a relaxing non-stimulating activity (like breathing or reading). Return when sleepy.
4️⃣ Using Alcohol, Caffeine, or Late Meals
❌ Alcohol = disrupts REM
❌ Caffeine after 2PM = destroys deep sleep
❌ Heavy dinners = interrupts recovery
🛠️ Coach Fix:
✅ Implement a "Recovery Cut-Off Time" (no food, caffeine, or screens 2-3 hours before bed).
✅ Promote magnesium-rich foods or calming teas instead.
5️⃣ Screens Before Bed / Blue Light Exposure
❌ Netflix, scrolling, TV = Melatonin blocker
🛠️ Coach Fix:
✅ Create a wind-down ritual 30–60 minutes before bed: breathing, stretching, reading, or journaling.
✅ Use blue light blockers or apps if screen use is needed.
6️⃣ Daytime Sedentary Lifestyle
❌ Low physical activity = Low sleep pressure
❌ No sunlight = Disrupted melatonin production
🛠️ Coach Fix:
✅ Encourage 10–15 min of morning sunlight exposure.
✅ Add movement snacks or walks throughout the day to increase sleep drive.
🎯 Coaching Goal:
Help them eliminate or reverse the perpetuating factors that are keeping the poor sleep cycle alive.
Because once you do that — the body often naturally restores sleep quality without needing medication or extreme interventions.
