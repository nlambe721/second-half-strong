Here is the philosophy and the core rules for the Sleep for Men Over 40 program, straight from the mindset of the Over 40 Alpha Brotherhood and Funk Roberts.
The Alpha Sleep Philosophy
1. Sleep is Your #1 Recovery Weapon For men over 40, sleep is the absolute foundation for fat loss, testosterone production, and overall health. You cannot out-train, out-eat, or out-supplement poor sleep. If your sleep is broken, your testosterone is broken.
2. Deep Sleep is the Anabolic Hormone Supercharger Approximately 90% of your daily testosterone is produced during deep sleep and REM sleep, primarily within your first 3 to 5 sleep cycles. This is when your body releases Growth Hormone, DHEA, and Luteinizing Hormone (LH) to rebuild muscle, burn belly fat, and restore your drive.
3. Sleep is a Relationship, Not a Transaction Most men try to fix sleep with pure willpower, treating it like a performance they need to "win". The Alpha philosophy flips the script: you do not force sleep; you build a relationship with it. Great sleep is the natural outcome of how you structure your day, your light exposure, and your nervous system regulation.
4. Rhythm is Recovery Your body thrives on a predictable 24-hour internal clock (your circadian rhythm). Consistency is far more important than perfection. When your body knows exactly when to power up and when to wind down, you build natural "sleep pressure" that results in deep rest.
5.Restorative Sleep is key - Most men over 40 should aim for 7–9 hours of total sleep per night, but the real focus should be achieving 3–4 hours of restorative sleep through Deep Sleep and REM Sleep. These sleep stages are responsible for testosterone production, muscle repair, fat loss, brain health, memory consolidation, immune function, and overall recovery. Prioritizing sleep quality is often more important than simply increasing total sleep duration.
The Funk Roberts Over 40 Alpha Sleep Rules
Rule 1: Anchor Your Wake Time and Morning Light Want to fix your nights? Start in the morning. Pick a realistic wake-up time and stick to it daily, aiming for no more than a 30-minute variation, even after a bad night. Within 30 to 60 minutes of waking, get outside for 5–10 minutes of natural sunlight to anchor your circadian rhythm.
Rule 2: Stimulus Control (The Bed is for Sleep and Sex Only) You must break the subconscious link between your bed and stress. Never scroll on your phone, answer emails, or watch TV in bed. This teaches your brain that the bed equals deep rest.
Rule 3: Execute the 20-Minute Rule If you are lying in bed awake and frustrated for about 20 minutes, get out of bed. Go to a low-light "calm zone" and do something boring, like reading fiction or deep breathing, and only return to bed when you feel sleepy again.
Rule 4: Master the Alpha Wind-Down Routine Sleep starts 30 to 60 minutes before your head hits the pillow. You must create psychological distance between your busy day and your night by shutting down all screens and blue light 60 minutes before bed. Switch to dim lamps or red lights, and use this time to stretch, journal, or drink magnesium tea.
Rule 5: Optimize Your Thermal and Light Environment To fall into deep sleep, your core body temperature must drop. Keep your bedroom temperature between 60–67°F (16–19°C). Make the room pitch black using blackout blinds or a sleep mask, and eliminate all electronics and LED lights.
Rule 6: Time Your Nutrition and Caffeine What you put in your body during the day dictates your sleep at night. Cut off all caffeine by 1 PM or 2 PM. Finish your last large meal 2 to 3 hours before bed so your body can focus on deep recovery instead of digestion. If you need a nighttime snack, opt for a small amount of protein and healthy fats (like Greek yogurt or walnuts) to stabilize your blood sugar.
Rule 7: Use the 4-2-6-2 Breathing Protocol Most men over 40 are stuck in "fight or flight" (sympathetic) mode. You must activate your "rest and digest" (parasympathetic) system before bed. Use the 4-2-6-2 nasal breathing technique for 5 to 10 minutes nightly: Inhale for 4 seconds, Hold for 2 seconds, Exhale for 6 seconds, Hold for 2 seconds. This mimics natural sleep breathing, lowers cortisol, and boosts melatonin.
Rule 8: Stop Trying to Sleep (Paradoxical Intention) Sleep performance anxiety will keep you wired. Instead of trying hard to fall asleep, gently aim to stay awake in a relaxed state. Remove the pressure by telling yourself, “I give myself permission to rest. I don’t have to force sleep. My body knows what to do”.
Rule 9: Deploy the Bad Night Bounce-Back Protocol You will have bad nights, but you cannot let them compound. If you get less than 5 hours of sleep, do not sleep in, do not over-caffeinate, and do not take long naps. Wake up at your normal time, get your morning sunlight, limit any naps to 15-20 minutes before 2 PM, and go to bed on your normal rhythm that evening
