5 VITAMIN D3 + K2
What it is:
Vitamin D3 is a fat-soluble vitamin that supports calcium absorption, bone
health, muscle function, and immune health. Vitamin K2 is a fat-soluble
vitamin that helps activate proteins involved in calcium use and bone
metabolism. Together, they are often paired in supplements aimed at bone,
cardiovascular, and overall healthy aging support.
Why men over 40 need it:
Men over 40, 50, and 60 are more likely to deal with low vitamin D status due
to indoor living, less sun exposure, northern climates, darker skin, excess body
fat, and aging-related lifestyle factors. As men get older, bone health, muscle

function, recovery, and long-term health become more important. D3 helps
support healthy vitamin D levels, and K2 is commonly paired with it as part of
a smart bone and calcium-support strategy.
Benefits:
Supports healthy vitamin D status, bone health, muscle function, immune
health, and healthy aging. It is especially valuable for men who spend most of
their time indoors, live in colder climates, or do not get regular sun exposure.

Vitamin K2: 90 to 180 mcg per day, often as MK-7

ideally be guided by a 25(OH)D blood test rather than guessing.
When to take it:
Take it with a meal containing fat, usually at breakfast or lunch for
consistency.

6 ESSENTIAL AMINO ACIDS (EAAS) / BCAAS
What they are:
Amino acids are the building blocks of protein. Essential amino acids (EAAs)
are the nine amino acids your body cannot produce on its own and must get
from food or supplements. BCAAs (branched-chain amino acids) are three of
those EAAs—leucine, isoleucine, and valine—that play a direct role in muscle
metabolism.
Why men over 40 need them:
As you get older, your body becomes less responsive to protein. This is called
anabolic resistance. That means it takes more stimulus to build and maintain
muscle. EAAs help directly trigger muscle protein synthesis, especially leucine,
which acts as the key signal for muscle growth. They are particularly useful for
29

men who train fasted, struggle to hit daily protein intake, or want to preserve
muscle while losing fat.
Benefits:
Supports muscle protein synthesis, helps preserve lean muscle, improves
recovery, and can reduce muscle breakdown during training or calorie
deficits. EAAs are more complete than BCAAs and provide a broader muscle-
supporting effect.
Dosage:
For most men, 8 to 12 grams of EAAs per serving is effective. If using BCAAs
alone, doses are typically 5 to 10 grams, but EAAs are the better overall choice
since they provide all essential amino acids needed for muscle building.
When to take them:
Before or during workouts, especially if training fasted. They can also be used
between meals or during long training sessions to support muscle retention
and recovery.

14 D+ (or NAD+ Boosters)
What it is:
NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every cell
in your body that plays a critical role in energy production, cellular repair, and
metabolic function. Because NAD+ itself is not easily supplemented directly,
most products use precursors like NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) or NR
(nicotinamide riboside) to help increase NAD+ levels.
Why men over 40 need it:
NAD+ levels naturally decline with age. This decline is linked to lower energy,
reduced cellular efficiency, slower recovery, and accelerated aging
processes. For men over 40, supporting NAD+ levels can help maintain energy
production at the cellular level and support long-term health and
performance.
Benefits:
Supports cellular energy production, may improve mitochondrial function,
43

supports recovery, and plays a role in healthy aging and longevity. Many men
report improved energy and mental clarity when using NAD+ precursors
consistently.
Dosage:
Typical dosing depends on the form. For NMN or NR, most supplements fall in
the range of 250 to 500 mg per day, with some protocols going higher. Start
on the lower end and assess response.
When to take it:
Usually taken in the morning, since it supports energy production and cellular
activity. It can be taken with or without food depending on the product.

15 ASHWAGANDHA
What it is: An adaptogenic herb used for stress, anxiety, sleep, and
sometimes male reproductive or testosterone-related positioning.
Why men over 40 need it: Stress, poor sleep, and cortisol-driven recovery
problems hammer men over 40. Ashwagandha fits better as a resilience and
sleep-support tool than as some miracle testosterone hack.
Benefits: Evidence suggests ashwagandha extracts may lower stress, anxiety,
and cortisol, and limited evidence suggests modest improvements in
testosterone and sperm quality in some men. NCCIH also notes that long-
term safety is not well established.
Dosage:
A practical range is 300 to 600 mg/day of a standardized extract.
When to take it:
Often in the evening if using it for stress and sleep, or split morning and
evening if using a twice-daily product.
